Managing

What are coping mechanisms?

Coping mechanisms help with managing stress, coping with anger, loneliness, anxiety, or depression. Coping styles and mechanisms can have positive or negative effects. Some coping mechanisms may be counterproductive and may create more problems down the line. These are called maladaptive coping mechanisms.

Examples of Healthy Adaptive Coping Mechanisms

  • Establishing a healthy support system
  • Giving yourself time to relax
  • Exercising regularly
  • Listening to music
  • Journaling your thoughts and feelings
  • Doing meditation

Seeking help from others

Do not be afraid to ask for help. You can seek support from family and friends. You may also seek professional or even join a support group Other people could be experiencing or have experienced the same problems and offer you valuable advice. You should not feel ashamed of seeking professional help. The fact your seeking help is a sign of strength not weakness. At first you may feel uncomfortable but over time you will be able to open up to them. They will give you helpful advice on how to cope and manage with your mental health concerns.
