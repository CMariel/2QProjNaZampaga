Avoiding

What are triggers?

A trigger is something that elicits a strong negative emotional reaction. They usually have a strong sensory connection or are connected to deeply ingrained habits (Cunic, 2020). It can be used in the context of trauma and PTSD, where people remember or relive a past traumatic event. It also applies in the context of other mental health illnesses such as eating disorders, anxiety, and substance abuse disorders. In this case, a trigger can cause the increase or return of symptoms. They can be internal or external (Cunic, 2020). Internal triggers are from memories and emotions while external triggers come from the environment. External triggers are the ones that can be avoided.

Trigger Warnings

Trigger warnings ready people for potential triggers in discussions or content (GoodTherapy, 2018). They are a good practice because they keep in mind that some people may feel uneasy due to some content. They help people prepare themselves physically and mentally for potential triggers and avoid them if they know they are not yet ready. Avoiding triggers can give a person control over his/her situation especially when their mental health issues are related to feelings of confusion, anxiety, and the like. Avoiding triggers will not necessarily treat the underlying mental health concerns but are helpful to ensure that the individual will not be surprised by something they are not yet ready to face. Triggers should be avoided if an individual is not yet ready. If avoiding triggers is affecting your life too much, that means it is time to seek help.
